Class Participation Self-Evaluation

English 314: African Literature

Your Name:_______________________________

Class participation is one of the most important parts of this course. Your class participation grade (100 pts) is made up of literature circles (50 pts), Confer (20 pts), film festival (15 pts), and the counterpart assignment (15 pts). Extra credit is available for your own independent research of African culture and issues (10 pts). Film festival attendance and independent work should also be reported on Nicenet. This self-evaluation does not include your class attendance. (As stated on the syllabus, if you missed three or fewer classes it will have no impact on your final grade.)

Literature Circles:

The literature circle assignment took place in nine class meetings over five full weeks of class time. Over this time the syllabus states that students were to read four to five novels of about 200 pages each. One novel was supposed to provide a Euro/American perspective on Africa, and a film from the same perspective was also to be viewed. Students were responsible for identifying and obtaining their own books, reading independently, coming to class prepared to discuss the works, and making a supportive contribution to their group.

Film Festival:

The first sentence of our syllabus states that "This course will examine the realities of contemporary Africa primarily through a study of modern African literature and film." While not all films at the Franco-phone film festival addressed Africa, any of those films could be counted toward the expectation of viewing three films (two shorts = one feature length). Students who could not attend the festival were invited to view African films on their own from Sangren film/video library. Comments on the films viewed should be posted on Nicenet.
