Take Home Final Essay

This final assignment is meant to be an opportunity to reflect back on what you have learned in English 480 by comparing what we have learned in our class with observations of the real world, with books that could have been used as texts for the class, or with syllabi of similar courses at other universities. Choose ONE of the options below. Be careful to draw on and refer to the specific learning that too place in 480 as you write the final essay, including traditional methods, reader response approaches, cultural studies, and new technologies. (Minimum 5 pages)

Option 1: Classroom Visit

Visit a secondary literature or language arts classroom and observe one class period on at least two different days or two different class periods on the same day. Interview the teacher asking questions about his or her approach to teaching literature, curriculum, discussion leading, activities, etc.

For your essay, describe the class, students, and curriculum. Drawing on what you have learned in 480, what seem to be the strength's and weaknesses of the teacher's approach? If you were this teacher's intern how would you proceed? Include any data collected.

Through your classroom visits, interview, and write up, demonstrate a high level of professional conduct, awareness of the complexity of the teaching process, and respect for the expertise and confidentiality of the teacher your observe.

Option 2: English Teaching Textbook

Drawing on what you have learned in English 480, read and analyze a book that could have been used as a textbook for our class. To further acquaint yourself with the professional literature in English education, choose a book new to you, one you have not read for another class. Some of these books have been ordered as optional texts for 480 (see those in blue) and all can be ordered on-line.. These books can also be found for free in Sangren library. Suggested titles:

 

Adolescent Literature as a Compliment to the Classics Vol. I, II, III. (1995-7).

Critial Encounters in High School English, Deborah Applebee, (NCTE, 2001)

Exploring and Teaching the English Language Arts. Stephen Tschudi and Diana Mitchel (Longman, 1999).

Explorations in the Teaching of English. Tchudi, Stephen. (HarperCollins, 1989).

How English Teachers Get Taught: Methods of Teaching the Methods Class. Samgorinsky and Whiting. (NCTE, 1995).

How Porcupines Make Love III: Readers, Texts, Cultures. Alan Purves (1995)

Language and Reflection: An Integrated Approach to Teaching English. Anne Ruggles Gere (Macmillian, 1992).

Making the Journey: On Being and Becoming a Teacher of the English Language Arts. Leila Christenbury. (Boynton/Cook 1993).

Rationales for Challenged Books. CD-ROM (NCTE 1998).

Reading, Writing, and Rising Up. Linda Christensen (Rethinking Schools, 2000).

Reshaping High School English. Pirie, Bruce. (NCTE 1997).

Standards in Practice Grades 9-12. Smagorinsky. (NCTE: 1996).

Standards in Practice Grades 6-8. Wilhelm. (NCTE: 1996).

Teaching Literature in the Secondary School. Beach and Marshall. (Harcourt 1993)

Teaching English in Middle and Secondary Schools. Rhoda Maxwell and Mary Jordan Meiser. Second Edition (Prentice Hall, 1997).Crossing Over: Whole Language for Secondary English Teachers. Foster, Harold. (Harcourt Brace 1994).

The English Teacher's Companion: A Complete Guide to Classroom, Curriculum, and the Profession. Jim Burke (Boynton / Cook, 1999).

Uncommon Sense. Mayher, J.S. (Boynton/Cook, 1990).

Uncovering the Curriculum: Whole Language in Secondary and Postsecondary Classrooms. Strickland (1993).

Weaving in the Women. Whaley and Dodge. (NCTE. 1998).

Weaving a Virtual Web Ed. Sibylle Gruber (NCTE,2000)

You Gotta be the Book: Teaching Engaged and Reflective Reading with Adolescents. Wilhelm, Jeff. (Teacher's College Press, 1997).

What are the strengths and weaknesses of this book? What does the textbook include that our course did not? What did our course include that was not in the textbook? Did our "experimental, internet-based student-led" approach in 480 "cover the material" as well as or more effectively as this textbook does? Based on your analysis of this book, what recommendations would you make for English 480 next term?

Option 3: Compare and Contrast On-Line Syllabi
Closely examine on-line syllabi for courses similar to English 480. What are the strengths and weaknesses of several of these courses? What are the similarities and differences between these courses? What do other courses include that our course did not? What did our course include that was not in the courses described on the web? What would you like to learn from students who took this other class? What could they learn from you? Based on your analysis of these syllabi, what recommendations would you make for English 480 next term?
