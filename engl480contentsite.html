Content-based, Cultural Studies Website

Working collaboratively, you will design a content-based, cultural studies website that can serve as a resource for secondary English students and teachers around the world. This site will give you a chance to study literary, library, and internet resources, design effective and relevant curriculum, create teaching and evaluative materials, and reflect on and contribute to the content of secondary literature instruction. While this assignment will create skills and resources for the teaching of the future, it also builds strongly on traditional practice and will prepare you for teaching in non-technological environments as well.

You will enter your site on the Thinkquest server -- a world famous location for educational content-based websites. This year ten universities have been invited to participate in this collaboration with Thinkquest, including WMU. Thinkquest is one of the most innovative and academically meaningful internet teaching and learning programs in the world and its sites are visited by thousands of students and teachers everyday. For more information from Thinkquest, here is a brief description of the program and the complete guidelines and criteria to which Thinkquest would like to see your site aspire. Here are tips on thinkquest sites developed by a professor at Eastern Connecticut University.

Collaborative Work

Group learning, team teaching, and collegial collaboration are a crucial part of teaching. Facilitating student learning calls for a deep understanding of the group learning process. Thus a primary objective of English 480 is for all of us to develop and practice our collaborative knowledge and skills. Preparing content-based websites will require extensive time and effort including wide reading, library research, internet research, writing,web publishing and web design, group discussion and decision making, and assessment activities. All students are expected to take responsibility for group work while cooperating with others in a professional, mutually respectful, supportive, fair, and critically reflective way.

Site Topics

Your group should select one of the following topics. The topics are broad and encompassing; they could compliment many different secondary courses and complete courses could be taught on any one of them. As a grou carefully decide how to limit and focus in order to be most effective. You may want to give your unit a new, more focused title.

Content Expectations

1. Address literary works. Every unit must extensively address several literary works that could be taught in a secondary English classroom (7-12) as well as whatever other works such as books, essays, articles, videos, websites, etc. that are deemed relevant. Your content-based site must address both young adult literature and literary works from the traditional canon. Feel free to incorporate literature available on-line as well as libraries and traditional course texts.

2. Utilize a cultural studies approach that helps students understand themselves, the literary works, and literary, cultural, social, political, and historical contexts.

3. Provide accurate and useful information and resources for students and teachers. Students will appreciate relevant and attractive information, opportunities to express their viewpoints and creativit, as well as intellectually challenging ideas. Teachers will appreciate sample syllabi, study questions, writing prompts, reader-response activities, project ideas, quizes and tests, rubrics, sample letters to parents, etc.

4. Make the site interactive. Include several webquests and a discussion and/or message board. What other interactive ideas can you come up with?

5. Address the diverse needs of learners. How would 7th graders and 12th graders approach the site differently? How will you support students who are poor readers as well as those who are academically advanced? How will your site address different learning styles? How will your site be relevant to students from different social, cultural, and geographic backgrounds?

Class Presentation of Your Site

Your group will have one class period in which to present your site to the rest of the class and orient us to it use. You may assign reading (up to 100 pages) to the class before your presentation. You should request written feedback on your site--this feedback could be given on the class computer conference. Although some formal presentation of the site is acceptable, do devise interactive and involving ways for classmates to learn about and critique your site.

Technology

This content-based site is intended to develop the internet literacy and web-publishing skills of all students. Dreamweaver is available on all campus lab computers and for free 30 day trial down load. You can also use Netscape Composer which is free on all machines with Netscape. The WMU IT Lab in Sangren Hall can give individual students help.

Thinkquest Specifics

Thinkquest instructions: Ateam can be created, set up, and the proposal submitted by the faculty coach all at one time. The coach will enter the names of the student team members, who do not need to register. We are recommending that a faculty coach select, or have the team select a team leader for each team. The coach would then invite the team leader to register as the second coach on the team. This person would then have the ability to access the team admin page to make any needed changes to the team without involving the faculty coach. The ability to view a team's Web site without needing a password during the development period. The Web sites will not be listed publicly until the faculty coach chooses to do so, so that they will remain essentially private while under development. But the faculty coach, or others the team wishes to review their site can do so without needing to use a password. A password would still be required to upload files or make changes to the sites.

Thinkquest has provided a "resource center" with helpful supporting information. Their resource list page; the key link on this page is probably the "Uploading Tutorial." They also have a help desk that can answer questions.
