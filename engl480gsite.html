Group Web Site

In the Group Web Site assignment your student-led group will not only be researching, designing, and presenting to your classmates cutting-edge teaching approaches, but you will be designing an interactive web site to bring these same teaching approaches to other preservice and inservice teachers around the world.

As you research your topic and consider ways to impact the future teaching of your classmates, the group web site also asks you to consider how you can impact the teaching of the entire teaching profession. The question foremost in your mind as you design your group site ought to be: How can we help other teachers learn about and come to integrate the approach that we advocate? You may find that involving one or more currently practicing teachers in the development of your site may give you valuable feedback and ideas. You will want to think about the style of learning and the interactive possibilities that the internet makes possible and incorporate them into your site. The goal is to create a site that will help preservice and practicing teachers everywhere learn how to integrate your topic into their curriculum. Feel free to focus the site for teachers in your chosen content area and grade level.

You will enter your site on the Thinkquest server -- a world famous location for educational content-based websites. This year ten universities have been invited to participate in this collaboration with Thinkquest, including WMU. Thinkquest is one of the most innovative and academically meaningful internet teaching and learning programs in the world and its sites are visited by thousands of students and teachers everyday. For more information from Thinkquest, here is a brief description of the program and the complete guidelines and criteria on to which the group site you are creating should aspire.

2. GETTING STARTED The proceedures will be finalized by Sept. 7. Here is what we know at present:
A team can be created, set up, and the proposal submitted by the faculty coach all at one time. The coach will enter the names of the student team members, who do not need to register. We are recommending that a faculty coach select, or have the team select a team leader for each team. The coach would then invite the team leader to register as the second coach on the team. This person would then have the ability to access the team admin page to make any needed changes to the team without involving the faculty coach. The ability to view a team's Web site without needing a password during the development period. The Web sites will not be listed publicly until the faculty coach chooses to do so, so that they will remain essentially private while under development. But the faculty coach, or others the team wishes to review their site can do so without needing to use a password. A password would still be required to upload files or make changes to the sites. We expect to have these changes finalized and tested and ThinkQuest pre service ready for coaches to sign up their teams and get Web accounts by September 7th.
