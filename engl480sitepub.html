Publishing your Web Pages

on WMU Unix Server

 

Introduction

The Information Technology department at WMU has been hard at work attempting to make web publishing easier for students. Unfortunately, some of the changes have actually made things more complicated, depending on which html software you are using. On this page I will update what I know about web publishing on the WMU server with the most specific help I can give. Soon WMU will release the address of an official site to help students publish web pages. If and when you find my or their instructions inadequate, don't hesitate to call the University Help Desk, 387-5161 -- you pay for their service with your tuition and fees!

Teachers and aspiring teachers may want to examine my advice for creating interactive teaching web portfolios.

 

WMU Server or Free Server??

There are other ways to publish beside using the WMU server, and there are many good free sites. (A few free sites include: geocities, homestead, alta vista, and aol. ) Free sites have the advantage that they may be easier to use and come with prepackaged web publishing pieces, such as templates, backgrounds, menu bars, buttons, images, counters, visitor books, etc. At free sites you can usually upload pages you have developed with your own html software as well using their templates. As a future teacher it is good to be familiar with using free sites since it then be easy for you to support your students' publishing and creating their own web pages on the free sites.

Free sites also have disadvantages. First off, they throw advertising on to your web site every time pages are opened. The more the site is used the more advertising seems to come up. Also, they have your email address and may send advertising to your email. While it may be a bit more work to publish on the WMU server, you do learn a bit better the mechanics of web publishing. At the present time pages published on the WMU server are supposed to remain active for nine months after graduation. At that time you could move your site to a free server or to a school district server (assuming you are employed as a teacher.) Once your site is created it should not be difficult to move it. Remember to keep a copy of all of your pages on your own hard or floppy disk.

Setting Up Your WMU Web Folder

The first step to publishing on the WMU server is to create a "www," world wide web, folder on the Unix server. So, you will need a Unix account name and password. WMU students can take their bronco card to the Bernhard Center or the Computer Center to get their unix account and password. (When you log on the first time you will have to change your password.)

To create your www folder you need to talk directly to the Unix server. You do this using a "telnet" program. This program allows you to give specific instructions to the unix server regarding your account. Begin by:

1. Opening "NCSA Telnet" (or any other telnet program).

2. Hostname: unix.cc.wmich.edu or bronco.cc.wmich.edu (both names go to the same server) (You may need to open new connection.)

3. select "unix"

4. at "log in" type your user name

5. type your password

6. at "~>" type: makeweb (all lower case, no space, hit return) This will create your www folder.

7. at "~>" type: pwd (unix will list your pathway, write it down carefully) This will give you the full pathway to your folder. You will need this pathway to use the automatic publishing command with Netscape Composer, although with Dreamweaver you do not need the full pathway. Note: if you find that pages you are publishing can not be viewed it may be because the computer people have moved your pages and you need to check your pathway again with the pwd command.)

8. type "exit"

Naming Your Pages

Be sure that your home page has the name "index.html." When your account is viewed this index page will then be the first account that comes up. When creating links to other pages that will be stored in your www folder all you need for the address is the name of the page, eg. "lessonplans.html." For links to pages outside your own www folder, you need to put the full well address starting with "http://." Often publishing problems are caused by names not matching. When you update any page or image you must publish over the existing page by using the exact same name.

Publishing Your Pages

Publishing your pages means that you are basically transfering them from your local disk to the web folder on your WMU account. This means using an "FTP" or "File Transfer Protocal" program. Many html software programs, such as Netscape Composer and Dreamweaver, have FTP programs built into them. When you publish with a built-in program you can set it so that all associated image and graphics files are published simultaneously. If you use a stand alone FTP program (there are many freeware versions for PCs, for Macs use "Fetch") you will need to be sure all image and graphic files are also uploaded or else your page will have broken puzzle piece icons or other errors.

If you are using Netscape Composer:

    1. Go to the file command "publish"

    2. Input the full file pathway. Example:

ftp://unix.cc.wmich.edu/home10/stugrp8f/UNIXUSERNAME/www/index.html (Note: use "ftp" not "http" when publishing)

If you are using Dreamweaver 3 or later.

1. Go to the command "publish."

2. Input the address of the unix server, "unix.cc.wmich.edu" or "bronco.cc.wmich.edu"

3. Once the program has located your www folder, find your local folder and push the arrow button to put your file up. (Be sure you have set the command to publish associated pages.)

If you are using a stand alone FTP program:

1. Establish a new connection with the "unix.cc.wmich.edu" server and the www folder at your user name ("bronco.cc.wmich.edu" is the same server).

2. Find the files you want to publish on your local disks, remembering that all associated image or graphic files will need to be published separately.

Making Your Pages Viewable

At the present time any student pages that are going to be viewable need to have the privacy permissions changed. The first time that needs to be done by the computer center and teachers need to provide a list of names to the IT office so pages can be made viewable. Next, ever time after publishing a new page or graphic image, the privacy permission must be turned off for that page. That is accomplished by returning to the Telnet program described above under "Setting Up Your WMU Web Folder". At the" ~>" prompt type "fixweb." The Information Technology people promise that soon this proceedure will no longer be necessary.

Viewing Your Pages

Use Netscape (or other browser) to verify that your page is published. Your web address is: http://unix.cc.wmich.edu/~UNIXUSERNAME. The index page comes up with just your user name; other pages follow after your username and a slash, for example, http://unix.cc.wmich.edu/~acareywe/York.html would be the address for a page I published titled "York.html."
