High School Content Expectations released for
English Language Arts!

New standards and curriculum expectations for high school English in Michigan have been released by the Michigan Department of Education.

These are some of the finest language arts standards in the United States. They reflect research and best practice in language arts content and instruction. They protect teacher decision making and are not narrow, highly restrictive, grade-level-by-grade-level standards being forced on teachers and students in many states by No Child Left Behind. The standards do not include a list of required readings for every student.

In the words of the opening of the document, these standards are "intended to support conversatoins at the school and district level." They are "comprehensive" though "not meant to be used as a proportional guide to curriculum development... teachers are not expected to spend equal time on each standard."

The proposed standards are available at: MDE High School Language Arts Standards. In addition to high quality standards, What do secondary English teachers really want?
