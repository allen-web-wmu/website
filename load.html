Loading Your Site.

There are three (3) ways to load your site to homepages. (We are only going to elaborate on homepages, since this is the platform that the IT lab uses. Other platforms will be difficult to get help with.)

First thing's first, create your site.

1. Click on "Site" on your top toolbar. Go to "Define Sites." Give your site a name. (The title of your index page is good. Or your last name.) Choose your root by clicking on the folder icon (next to "Root"). It is a good idea to use a zip disk, since the network or your computer will freeze quite a bit. By using a disk and SAVING OFTEN, you will not lose precious hours of work.

2. Now, on your left window, choose "Remote Info." It will have this weird little "Server Access" box. Choose "FTP" preference. Only enter the following information: "Host: homepages.wmich.edu" "Username: a4carey" (this is an example name). DO NOT ENTER YOUR PASSWORD HERE, IT WILL SAVE IT TO THE HARD DRIVE. You will have an opportunity to enter it later. Choose "Passive FTP" as a preference at the bottom, you will incur fewer errors when loading.

3. Click "OK." Click "Done."

Site window will appear-- don't close!!* The Local Server is your disk on the right.
The Remote Server is homepages on the left.

First Way to Load Your Site: (This is the ideal way to load your site in its entirety--or several pages at one time).
1. Connect to the server. (This is where it will ask for your password).
2. Select the files you would like to load in your local server window.
3. Click on the blue "up" arrow, this means "Put" in loading language.
4. ALWAYS INCLUDE DEPENDENT FILES--otherwise you will lose cute little pictures and links.
5. It will spin, think, and then you should be up in the server. If you receive an error, be sure that it is not simply due to the server being down. If it is a larger problem than the server, visit the IT lab.
6. View your site in both Netscape and Internet Explorer to be sure you've got what you want.

Second Way to Load Your Site: (An individual page by page way).
1. Save your page. (If you don't, it may load an earlier draft).
2. Go to "Site" on your top toolbar. Scroll to "put." This will prompt a connection to the server (if not currently connected).
3. ALWAYS INCLUDE DEPENDENT FILES.
4. View your site in both Netscape and Internet Explorer to be sure you've got what you want.

Third Way to Load Your Site: (The low-maintenance way).
1. Save your page. (If you don't, it may load an earlier draft).
2. Click on the up/down, "Put/ Get" arrows. This will prompt a connection to the server (if not currently connected).
3. ALWAYS INCLUDE DEPENDENT FILES.
4. View your site in both Netscape and Internet Explorer to be sure you've got what you want.

 

If you have already loaded your site, and you are reloading, make sure to click "Refresh" in your browser to get the most recent edition.
