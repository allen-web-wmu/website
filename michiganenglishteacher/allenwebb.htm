Dr. Allen Webb

Professor of English and English Education, Western Michigan University

 

I was a high school English teacher for six years and hold advanced certification in English and Social Studies, 7-12. During that time I earned an MA degree in English Education. I then embarked on an MA and Ph.D. in Comparative Literature, in English, Spanish, and French.

 

Thirteen years in my current position, I am a full professor of English at Western Michigan University focusing on English education, especially the teaching of literature. At WMU I have taught the capstone courses for English majors in the secondary curriculum in literature and compostion pedagogy to probably around 1000 English teachers, the majority of them taking positions in Michigan, as well as working with many practicing teachers in our MA and Ph.D. programs. I also have taught a wide range of literature courses at undergraduate and graduate levels.

 

I have written and published three books, one in literary studies and two on teaching literature, and am working on two book projects at the moment, one on doctoral education in English education and one on integrating technology and the internet into English teaching. I have published many articles, given zillions of conference presentations on the teaching of English, and a number of times served as a conference keynote speaker.

 

I have a 25-year history of activity in the National Council of the Teachers of English and its state affiliates; I am currently on the Executive Committee of the Conference on English Education (the professional organization for professors of English education) and on the Executive Committee of the Michigan Council of the Teachers of English (I am co-editor of their publication, Language Arts Journal of Michigan).

 

Many years ago I served on a MDE-MCTE committee that wrote state standards for English Language Arts that led eventually to the current standards and benchmarks. I also served on a state committee that formed the Language Arts teacher certification tests in Michigan.

 

I have been the principal investigator or co-PI on a number of education related grants, including $1.4 million US Dept. of Education "PT3" grant and I regularly direct research projects on the teaching of English in secondary schools.

 

I am also the father of one graduate of MI public schools and one current high school student.
