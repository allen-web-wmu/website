On-Line Literature Lesson Ideas

In addition to webquests these on-line learning activities represent the cutting edge of teaching literature with the internet and can generate intellectually and academically meaningful lesson plans.

1. Literary Archive

Closely examine an on-line literature archive. Some of these archives are actually collections of archives--explore to find an archive you would like to work with. Conceive of a creative and meaningful lesson plan for students to study or use this archive. Sample archives include: Rossetti Archive, Dickinson Electronic Archives, MIT Internet Classics, Rutgers Literary Resources on the Internet, Blake Archives, Bartleby, University of Virginia Electronic Text Center, Project Gutenberg, Early English Books On-line, Humanities Text Initiative, other on-line text resources.

2. Educational MOO

Study Robert Rozema's resource on educational MOOs, especially the section on frequently asked questions. Choose an existing on-line MOO, or imagine a MOO that you would like to create in relation to a particular work of literature and design a lesson plan for students to use this MOO.

3. E-Zine

Look at a variety of ezines. You could start with the On-line Library Ezine Index, Babel, and look at Amanda Warren's Ezine suggestions. But also be sure to examine some of the most innovative such as bornmagazine and Alt-X. Or ezines related to our course such as Kairos, Look at resources to develop ezines, such as Zinebook or Develop a lesson plan for students that involves either existing ezines, or an ezine that you would like your students to create.

4. Blogging

Look at a variety of on-line blogging sites. That could include: LiveJournal.com, Blogger.com, Look at the Live Journal of one of my former students, Bill Briger. Consider the possibility of using blogging for fictional characters. Develop a lesson plan related to a literary work that uses blogging or a blogging-like site or activity.

5. Video Game / Cyber Culture Analysis

Study high immersion games, on-line, playstation 2, x-box, etc. such as Everquest, Grand Theft Auto: Vice City, Myth, Myst, etc. and develop a lesson plan for students to examine games as literary and cultural documents.
