Santander is the capital of the province of Cantabria, on the Atlantic Coast of northern Spain.

 

The tourist brochures describe the place in this way:

The city of Santander , the community's capital, is not only on the coast of the Bay of Biscay, but is also very close to the Cantabrian Mountain Range, where the impressive Peaks of Europe are to be found. The city enjoys a mild climate, and this is so even in the summer thanks to the sea breezes. During the summer months there is a host of leisure activities: open air concerts, local festivities, the International Festival, bullfighting...

Santander is easily reached by sea,on the twice-weekly ferry from Plymouth. From the French border (Hendaye) there is a motorway coach service (around 220 kilometres), which takes only 2.5 hours. The airport, a mere 10 minutes from the city centre, connects Santander with Madrid and Barcelona

The University of Cantabria, which meets the region's needs in higher education, consists of some 14,000 students, a teaching staff of 900, and approximately 450 administrative workers. It enjoys one of the most favourable staff/student ratios within Spain, and now ranks third out of all Spanish Universities regards quality and standards of research according to the National Planning and Assessment Agency.
