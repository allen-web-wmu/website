Voter Information

Vote Tuesday, Nov. 6

Polls in Michigan are open 7 a.m. to 8 p.m.

Information for voting in Michigan is provided as a service to the Western Michigan University community and the public. Complete voter information is available online from the Michigan Secretary of State.

Important facts for WMU students

  • You can only vote in person where you are registered to vote. If you are registered to vote in Bloomfield Hills—3rd Precinct, Bloomfield Township, Oakland County—that is the only place you can vote in person.
  • College students are eligible to register and vote in their college town. WMU students are eligible to register using their local address on campus or in one of the area communities.
  • Michigan law requires the same address be used for voter registration and driver’s license purposes. If the address you provide on the voter registration application differs from the address shown on your Michigan driver's license or personal identification card, the state will automatically change your driver's license or state ID card address to match the address entered on the application. You will be mailed an address update sticker for your driver's license.
  • Changing the address at which you are registered to vote (and on your driver's license) does not affect your parents’ ability to claim you as a dependent or include you on their auto, health or other insurance.
  • If you change the address on your Michigan driver's license, the state will automatically change the address where you are registered to vote. If you registered to vote in Kalamazoo two years ago and have since changed the address on your driver’s license to Lansing, you are no longer registered to vote in Kalamazoo.
  • Do you know if you're registered to vote, and where? Are you sure? It takes less than 30 seconds to find out online where you’re registered to vote.
  • Tuesday, Oct. 9, is the deadline to register to vote in the Nov. 6 general election or change the address at which you are registered.
  • You must be a U.S. citizen and at least 18 years old on Election Day to vote.
  • You can register to vote at age 17 provided you will be 18 on or before Election Day.
  • Unless you registered in person at your clerk's office, you cannot vote absentee by mail in your first election.

Example: If you are registered to vote in Saginaw, did not register in person at your clerk’s office, and this is your first election in Michigan, you have only three options.

  1. Register to vote using your Kalamazoo area address and vote in Kalamazoo.
  2. Drive to Saginaw Election Day and vote in person.
  3. Go to your clerk’s office in Saginaw and request an absentee ballot in person.
